Interesting. Some strange choices here. Why does PRIMARY KEY need NOT NULL - isn't that part of the
definition of PRIMARY KEY?

And what is all this "deferrable" stuff. Ah, it allows broken references to be ignored during a
transaction. Then they'll be checked at the end of the transaction. Biggest use is circular
references, which we don't have. Let's get rid of that.
